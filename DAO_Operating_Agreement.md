# Operating Agreement of KDA Community DAO LLC

---
**Draft only for review **
---

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") governs the operations of **KDA Community DAO LLC**, a decentralized autonomous organization formed under the laws of the State of Wyoming, **subject to Title 17, Chapter 29 of the Wyoming Statutes, the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act (“the Act”), and Title 17, Chapter 31**.  

**The DAO is algorithmically managed** in accordance with **Article 17‑31‑109** of the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act, meaning that governance and operational decisions are executed automatically or semi-automatically through smart contracts rather than by human managers, subject to Member participation as defined in this Agreement.

### Notice of Restrictions on Duties and Transfers

The rights of Members in a decentralized autonomous organization may differ materially from the rights of Members in other limited liability companies.  
The Wyoming Decentralized Autonomous Organization Supplement, underlying smart contracts, Articles of Organization, and this Operating Agreement, if applicable, may define, reduce or eliminate fiduciary duties and may restrict:

- transfer of ownership interests,  
- withdrawal or resignation from the decentralized autonomous organization,  
- return of capital contributions, and  
- dissolution of the decentralized autonomous organization.

---

## 1. Formation

The Members confirm the formation of **KDA Community DAO LLC** (the “DAO”) pursuant to **Title 17, Chapter 29; Title 17, Chapter 31; and the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act (“the Act”)**.

The Articles of Organization were filed with the Wyoming Secretary of State on [DATE].

The DAO operates primarily through blockchain-based smart contracts.

---

## 2. Purpose

The purpose of the DAO is to engage in any lawful activity for which a decentralized autonomous organization may be organized in the State of Wyoming, including, but not limited to:

1. Providing advisory, consulting, or support services to other entities such as the [Foundation Name] or other affiliated organizations;  
2. Ensuring and maintaining the minimal operational functioning of the Kadena blockchain and its associated governance smart contracts;  
3. Engaging in any other lawful activity as determined by governance.

---

## 3. Blockchain

The official blockchain of the DAO is the **Kadena blockchain**.

All governance actions, voting records, and operational smart contracts of the DAO are intended to operate on the Kadena blockchain.

If multiple networks or forks of the Kadena blockchain exist, the canonical chain shall be the one recognized by governance of the DAO.

---

## 4. Smart Contracts

The governance and operational rules of the DAO are implemented through smart contracts deployed on the Kadena blockchain.

**Primary governance contract:**
- `dao.voting`

Additional DAO contracts may exist within the **`dao` namespace**, including but not limited to contracts used for governance, treasury management, or proposal execution.

All contracts deployed within the `dao` namespace and recognized by governance shall be considered part of the DAO’s operational infrastructure.

**Upgradability:**  
The DAO's smart contracts are designed to be **updatable, modifiable, or otherwise upgraded** as required by **W.S. 17-31-104(d)** and governance decisions.

**Order of precedence in case of conflict:**
1. Wyoming law (Title 17, Chapters 29 & 31, and the Act)  
2. Articles of Organization  
3. This Operating Agreement  
4. Smart contracts

---

## 5. Membership

Membership in the DAO is represented by ownership of governance tokens recognized by the DAO.

- A token holder becomes a Member of the DAO only upon participating in at least one governance vote recorded on the blockchain.
- Token holders who have never participated in a governance vote shall not be considered Members for the purposes of this Agreement.
- **Governance tokens confer voting rights** as defined herein and in the smart contracts but **do not grant equity ownership, profit shares, or liquidation preferences** unless explicitly approved by governance and in compliance with federal and state securities laws.

---

## 6. Governance

Governance decisions are made through on-chain voting using the governance mechanisms defined by the DAO’s smart contracts.

Unless otherwise specified, proposals pass with a simple majority of participating votes.

---

## 7. Officers

The Members may, through governance, appoint one or more Officers of the DAO.

- Officers may be appointed for a specific role, task, or operational responsibility as defined in the governance proposal that created the position.
- Each Officer appointment shall specify:
  - the scope of authority or task assigned  
  - the duration of the mandate  
  - any limitations or reporting obligations
- Officers act solely as agents of the DAO for the purposes defined by governance and do not acquire ownership or managerial authority beyond the mandate granted to them.
- Officers may be removed or replaced at any time through governance.

### 7.b Safe Harbor for Officers

- Officers of the DAO shall not be held liable for actions taken in good faith in the scope of their assigned duties.  
- Officers are only responsible for losses resulting from fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence.  
- Compliance with governance and reporting obligations as defined by the DAO constitutes acting in good faith.

---

## 8. Limited Liability

Members shall not be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the DAO solely by reason of being a Member.

---

## 9. Treasury

Digital assets owned by the DAO may be held in blockchain wallets controlled by smart contracts or multi-signature wallets approved by governance.

---

## 10. Assumption of Risk

Members acknowledge that participation in the DAO is voluntary and at their own risk.

Members further acknowledge that smart contracts may contain bugs, vulnerabilities, or unforeseen behaviors, and that interaction with such contracts may result in partial or total loss of digital assets.

The DAO and its Members shall not be liable for losses resulting from such technical risks.

---

## 11. No Fiduciary Duties

To the fullest extent permitted by law, no Member shall owe fiduciary duties to the DAO or to other Members solely by reason of being a Member.

Participation in governance or voting shall not create managerial or fiduciary obligations unless expressly established by governance.

---

## 12. No Partnership

Nothing in this Agreement shall be deemed to create a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship among the Members.

Members participate in the DAO solely through the governance mechanisms defined by its smart contracts.

---

## 13. Amendments

This Operating Agreement may be amended through a governance vote approved according to the rules defined in the DAO’s smart contracts.

---

## 14. Dissolution

- The DAO may be dissolved by governance vote according to the rules defined in the DAO’s smart contracts.
- The DAO shall also be considered dissolved if the Kadena blockchain permanently ceases to function or becomes inaccessible for an extended period of time such that governance through the DAO’s smart contracts can no longer be executed.
- Upon dissolution, remaining assets shall be distributed according to governance decisions and applicable law.

---

## 15. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Wyoming, specifically **Title 17, Chapters 29 & 31, and the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act (“the Act”)**.
